Sisig

Description

A sizzling dish of crispy pork, chicken liver, and eggs with a citrus dressing!

One of my favorite Filipino dishes!

Original recipe from Serious Eats

Ingredients

  • 1⁄4 lb chicken livers (optional)
  • 2 lbs skinless, boneless pork shoulder
  • 2 cups and 1 tbsp. canola oil, divided
  • 10 medium garlic cloves, minced
  • 1⁄2 medium red onion, finely diced
  • 1⁄4 cup soy sauce (preferably Filipino brands like Datu Puti or Silver Swan)
  • 3 tbsp. calamansi juice
  • 2 bird's eye chiles, stemmed, seeded, and minced
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 scallion, ends trimmed and sliced thinly on a bias, for garnish
  • Cooked white rice, for serving
  • Calamansi halves, for serving

Recipe

  1. If using chicken livers, place in a countertop blender and process until smooth. Transfer puréed livers to a small bowl, cover, and refrigerate.
  2. Blot pork shoulder dry with paper towels and cut into 1/2-inch pieces.
  3. Set a wire rack in a rimmed baking sheet lined with a double layer of paper towels. In a wok, heat 2 cups (475ml) oil over medium-high heat until it registers 375°F (190°C) on an instant-read thermometer. Working in 3 batches, carefully lower a third of the pork shoulder into the oil and fry until lightly browned all over, about 1 minute. Using a slotted spoon or spider, transfer pork to prepared rack. Return oil to 375°F (190°C) and repeat with remaining pork. Set aside.
  4. In a 12-inch cast iron skillet, heat remaining 1 tablespoon (15ml) oil over medium-high heat until shimmering. Add garlic and onion and cook, stirring frequently, until softened and golden brown in color, about 3 minutes.
  5. Add reserved fried pork, puréed chicken livers (if using), soy sauce, calamansi juice, and chiles, and mix until well combined.
  6. Increase heat to high and bring to a boil. Break eggs directly into pan, spacing them evenly around perimeter. Lower heat to maintain a simmer, cover, and cook, without stirring, until egg whites are opaque and yolks are runny but turning yellow on the edges, about 3 minutes.
  7. Add scallions and mix with pork and eggs until fully combined. Serve immediately with cooked white rice and calamansi halves.
